ABOUT CHILDREN HOME TENGKU BUDRIAH

BACKGROUND

Children Homes Tengku Budriah ranges of 9 Children's Home under the Administration of the Social Welfare Department of Malaysia that operates at 15 June 1964 and named after His Majesty Her Majesty the Queen of the 3rd, Tengku Ismail Tengku Budriah Binti late and he consented to officiate Children Homes at 25 September 1964. This house is located at Batu 4, Jalan Cheras 56000 Kuala Lumpur. This home consists of building blocks that are formed according to the 'cottage' to create a family life. The building which has an area of 3.43 hectares can accommodate 4 dormitories, 1 dining hall, an administration block and a baby room. Each dormitory seats up to 20 children while the baby room can accommodate up to 20 children. This concept, this house is very compact with four cottages hexagonal closely connected to each other with dormitories, a dining hall, administration block, baby room, bedroom nursery, surau, library, nursery activities.

MISSION

  • Determine to bring a new ray within the lives of the less fortunate with endeavor as much as possible all the necessary necessities they are
    in terms of either External or Spiritual needs
  • Guide them to become excellent human beings and successful in all aspects especially academically. Akan may be born from this
    Tengku Budriah Children's Home those who are awakened mentally, intellectually, physically and spiritually

VISION

  • Develop a welfare complex that serves as an additional channel that operates 24 hours and is consistent in helping the weak and difficult.
    Generate maximum funds through various programs that are organized to be sufficient to cover the welfare operations that want to be
    implemented

OBJECTIVE

  • Provide care and protection to ensure healthy physical and mental growth
  • Provides protection and recovery to the target group of the Department
  • Creating Caring Cultured society
  • Creating Wellness Community through Social Services

ADMISSION OF CHILDREN IN DEATH PARENTS

  • Children with mother or father received treatment
  • Children who are neglected and abandoned by the mother or father
  • Children where the parents is in jail or detention
  • Children who tortured or abused as physical, mental, sexual or emotional
  • Children who require care due to poverty, neglect and no care
